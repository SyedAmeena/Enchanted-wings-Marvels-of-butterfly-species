Marvels of Butterfly Species

Introduction

Butterflies, with their delicate wings and vibrant colors, have long captivated human hearts. This page explores some of the most enchanting species from around the world.

Featured Species

Monarch Butterfly (Danaus plexippus)

Known for its epic annual migration across North America, the Monarch travels thousands of miles.

Blue Morpho (Morpho peleides)

With iridescent blue wings native to Central and South American rainforests, this butterfly dazzles with reflection.

Swallowtail (Papilionidae)

Family Papilionidae offers species with graceful “tails” extending from their hindwings, often mimicking snakes or bird droppings to fool predators.

Peacock Butterfly (Aglais io)

Native to Europe and Asia, it has large “eyespots” on its wings that ward off predators by mimicking an owl’s gaze.
